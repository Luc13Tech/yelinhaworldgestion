FAA Type Certification Plan — 14 CFR Part 25
🏛
CERTIFICATION AUTHORITY: Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) · Aircraft Certification Office (ACO) · Standards: 14 CFR Part 25 (Airworthiness Standards: Transport Category) · DO-178C (Software) · DO-254 (Hardware) · DO-160G (Environmental). DER Structures in-house. EASA BASA validation planned in parallel.
Certification Phases
01
Pre-Application Meeting (PAM)
Initial FAA engagement. Present program overview, proposed cert basis, schedule. Identify ACO point of contact. Target: Q4 2026.
02
G1 Issue Paper — Certification Basis
Formally establish applicable 14 CFR Part 25 amendment level. Identify special conditions, equivalent safety findings, exemptions. Target: Q1 2027.
03
Certification Plan (CP) & MPS
Compliance method per regulation (test, analysis, inspection, similarity). Master Project Schedule with FAA milestones. Issue Papers for novel features (FBW, composite structure).
04
Avionics: DO-178C / DO-254 Plans
Software Development Plans for all DAL-A through DAL-D software (FCMC, FMS, TCAS, ELT). Hardware Design Assurance Plans for flight computers and electrical systems. FAA DER software approval.
05
Ground Tests & Structural Qualification
Static load tests (ultimate load, limit load). Fatigue & damage tolerance per Part 25.571. Ground Vibration Test (GVT). Systems integration tests. Fire resistance. Bird strike. Ditching.
06
Flight Test Authorization (FTA)
FAA approval for Experimental Airworthiness Certificate. Begin flight test program. Dedicated FAA Flight Test Engineer assigned. 3-aircraft fleet recommended.
07
Certification Flight Test Program
Full Part 25 compliance flight tests: performance (§25.101–115), stability & control, stall, high-altitude, cold soak, hot & high, systems, emergency, minimum control speeds, flutter, autopilot.
08
Type Certificate Awarded
All compliance findings closed. Compliance Checklist complete. Production Approval (PC/PAH). Airworthiness Directive surveillance baseline. Target: 2030. EASA BASA validation parallel.
